ABOUT ME

Hello, I'm Subash Neupane, a computer science student at Claflin University, deeply rooted in my Nepalese heritage. Throughout my academic journey, I've embraced various leadership roles that have shaped my passion for technology and community engagement. As a Mern Stack Developer, I have honed my skills and expertise, allowing me to contribute as an instructor for the National Society of Black Engineers on campus. This role has provided me with the opportunity to share my knowledge and foster a collaborative learning environment. In addition to my technical endeavors, I've held pivotal positions such as the Google Developer Student Club Lead and Vice President of the Global Student Organization. These experiences have not only broadened my horizons in technology but also strengthened my ability to lead and inspire others.


Furthermore, I proudly serve as a Career Ambassador, assisting fellow students in navigating their career paths and making informed decisions. As a fellow of EVhybridNoire, I am dedicated to promoting diversity and inclusivity within the tech industry, a cause that resonates with my values and aspirations. Beyond technology, I have a profound love for public speaking, relishing every opportunity to share my experiences and learn from the stories of others. A guiding principle in my life is encapsulated in this quote: "Always adhere to the process; the result is merely the culmination of that process." This mindset propels me forward, reminding me to focus on the journey, confident that the results will naturally follow.
